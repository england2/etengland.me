## What does a fear acclimation system need?


The fact that visual phobias can reliably be trigged by computer screens means that computer screens could be used to overcome them. Of course, this only stands if computers are used strategically!


A fear acclimation system is all about creating **gentle, managable discomfort**. If the user has their system shocked over and over by looking at things that terrify their nervous system, this will have the wrong effect!

To the idea is to look at something manageable, so to tell the brain that *"Yes, this visual symbol is a bit uncomfortable, but I'm fine; it's not a real threat"*. And when the user is fully comfortable with that stage, they turn the intensity of the visual phobia-trigger up one tiny notch.

Here's a overview of the visual phobia triggers one might choose for centipedes:
- Stage 1: Highly stylized and cartoonish clip art of centipedes.
- Stage 2: Detailed drawings of centipedes.
- Stage 3: Blurry, zoomed out pictures of real centipedes.
- Stage 4: Regular pictures of centipedes.
- Stage 5: Close-up pictures of centipedes.

It sounds goofy, but gradual exposure to certain fears over time teaches us that we're safe.

There's a few features our **CFAS** needs:
- ability to display phobia-triggers 
- ability to adjust the intensity of a phobia-trigger on the fly *(e.g blurring the image via mouse-wheel)*
- ability to organize phobia-triggers based on intensity
- ability to track progess, rank image-comfortability, and display the correct image
